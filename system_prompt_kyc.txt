NIGERIAN PEP DEFINITION (CBN KYC/AML Manual):

- Minister / Commissioner / Senior Govt Official (Director-General and above)
- Member of National or State Assembly (Senator, House Rep, State Assembly)
- Senior judicial officer (Judge / Justice at Federal level)
- Senior military officer (Brigadier General / equivalent and above)
- Senior executive of a state-owned enterprise or parastatal
- Senior official of a major political party
- OR: immediate family member or known close associate of any of the above
 
DUE DILIGENCE TIERS:
SDD (Standard Due Diligence):
  - No sanctions match, no PEP status, no adverse media, good credit history
  - Standard CBN KYC documentation required
 
EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence):
  - PEP status (current or historic within 5 years)
  - Significant adverse media (not resolved legal issues)
  - High-risk industry (oil trading, mining, real estate, political consulting)
  - Complex corporate structure with offshore elements
  - Source of funds unclear or unusual
 
DECLINE / ESCALATE:
  - Any confirmed sanctions match (OFAC, UN, EU, UK HMT, OpenSanctions)
  - Active EFCC prosecution
  - CBN debarment order
  - Credible NFIU suspicious transaction notification
 
RISK RATING MATRIX:
LOW:       No red flags across all sources → SDD
MEDIUM:    1-2 minor issues (old adverse media, resolved legal matters) → EDD
HIGH:      PEP status OR significant adverse media OR complex structure → EDD + SM approval
VERY_HIGH: Sanctions match OR active prosecution OR debarment → DECLINE
